Fuck long distance, we're having the time of our lives

This project is built with D3.js as a gift for my boyfriend as a celebration of our 3 years together.

The spiral

The idea of a spiral has been haunting me for a while now. I love circular visualisations and I'm fascinated by the beauty of the spiral's geometry. My inspiration came from tree rings: each year, another ring gets added to the trunk of the tree. By reading the texture and look of the rings, one can tell about its growth and its environment.

The d3.js spiral was made with the help of Wes Tyler's Spiral.js.

The data

One day my boyfriend and I thought it interesting to know how long we've been physically together. So we looked through our e-mails for plane tickets and other similar data. We were also curious about:

  • How many cities we visited together
  • How long was the longest period apart or the longest together
  • Who made most trips to visit the other

For this we compiled an Excel sheet with all these dates, and then we calculated those numbers. But it always felt it was missing something: it was missing its colour, its magic...

As our 3rd anniversary approached, I've been thinking about what would be the perfect celebration for it. I've already been brooding on the idea of visualising the Excel data for a while, so I decided to build it up.

However, the data was incomplete and in an improper form. I wanted to have an account of the cities we visited, together or individually. For that I had to look at meta-data from the photos we took in our travels.
